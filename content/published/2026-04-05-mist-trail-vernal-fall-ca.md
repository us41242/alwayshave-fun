## The Case for Going to Yosemite Right Now

Yosemite in July is not a secret. You know what else isn't a secret? How miserable it is — bumper-to-bumper on Tioga Road, full parking reservations required months out, shuttle lines at every trailhead. The valley turns into a really beautiful traffic jam.

April is different. The crowds haven't arrived, the waterfalls are running at maximum volume from snowmelt, and the temperature in the valley is in the mid-60s. Today's score for the Mist Trail: 100/100. Temperature 63°F, no rain, Good air quality. This is the version of Yosemite people go back for.

## Current Conditions

**Temperature:** 63°F in the valley. The Mist Trail earns its name — you will get wet from the waterfall spray well before you reach the top of Vernal Fall, and the mist creates its own microclimate that keeps the trail 5-10 degrees cooler than the surrounding area. Bring a light rain layer even if the sky is clear.

**Water flow:** Spring snowmelt means Vernal Fall is running at its most powerful. The 317-foot drop creates a wall of mist that you can see from the valley floor. The granite steps at the top of the fall (the famous ones everyone photographs) will be slick — take your time on them.

**Wind:** Calm. Good visibility.

**AQI:** Good. Smoke from Sierras wildfires is a real concern later in the season (July–October). April is clean.

## The Trail

Mist Trail to the top of Vernal Fall is 3 miles round trip with about 1,000 ft of gain. The first half is a wide, well-maintained path along the Merced River that feels easy. The second half — from the Vernal Fall footbridge to the top — is granite steps. A lot of granite steps. Cut into the rock directly, slick from spray, with a chain railing that you will use.

Most people stop at the footbridge, look at the view, and turn around. That's a fine hike. The people who go to the top of the fall have a different day — you come out onto a granite shelf overlooking the full drop with Nevada Fall visible above, Half Dome in the distance, and the Merced River canyon below. It's one of the genuinely great viewpoints in the park.

The extension to Nevada Fall adds another 2 miles and 900 ft of gain if you want a full day. I'd do it.

## Jake's Take

Two things that will make or break your day:

**Parking:** The valley parking lots fill before 9am on any decent weather day. Arrive before 8am or take the YARTS bus from Merced if you're coming from outside the valley. The day-use reservation system at peak season is a separate headache — check the Yosemite NPS site for current requirements before you drive out there.

**The mist:** The spray from Vernal Fall will soak you. I mean actually soak you — not light mist, not a sprinkle, waterfall spray that soaks your shirt from 50 feet away. In April this is refreshing. In October it's cold. Either way, put your phone in a dry bag before you hit the stone steps. Learned that one the hard way.

April in Yosemite, 100/100, falls at full volume, valley mostly empty. If you're within driving distance and don't have a reason not to, go this week.
